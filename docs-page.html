Customer Integration

Customer integration is the process of adding a new customer to our system. this process is done so that new customers can place their orders using our system.

Procedure

Here is the step by step procedure of Customer Integration into our system.
  1. Customers who want to order lenses approach our sales team.
  2. We sell our products in 3 different categories
    • Nova
    • VRX
    • Private Label (Customer's own branding)
  3. We send the list of our products and prices to the customer, the price of the product depends on the type of the product and with which branding he wants to buy the product.
  4. If the customer likes the products and their price then we start the customer integration process.
  5. Firstly, customers is asked to send their details to us, so we can start the customer integration process.
  6. After getting the customer details all the customer information is sent to the Accounts Department, the accounts team generates a unique customer code for the customer after verifying all the details.
  7. If the products ordered by the customer already exist in our system, then the Product Integration team will prepare a master excel of all the products ordered by the customer.
    • This master excel contains all the details related to the products ordered by the customers.
  8. If the product ordered by the customer is new and not exist in our system then the Product Integration team along with the Sales Team starts the designing and development of the new product, product integration team create the catalogue of the product and generates the new product code.
  9. The master excel created by the production integration team is sent to the pricing department to add pricing of the products, and also create the price in ERP.
  10. The pricing department sets the pricing of the products according to the customers and products.
  11. After this product order details are sent to 2 departments
    • Production Department
    • IT Department
  12. Production department enters product details into the Lensware software, Lensware software checks all the details and if everything is correct only then production of lens can be started
  13. IT team adds the product details to the ERP system
  14. At last the customer is asked to place a sample order to start the testing process.
  15. After the testing process is over, the customer can normally place orders using our system.

Inventory

Inventory refers to the products that are ready to sell, along with the raw materials that are used to produce them. There are three general categories of inventory, including raw materials (any supplies that are used to produce finished products), work-in-progress (WIP), and finished products or those that are ready for sale.

Inventory management allows businesses to minimize inventory costs as they create or receive products on an as-needed basis.

Inventory is divided according to two types of lenses

  • Stock Lens (Finished Lens)
  • RX Lens (Semi Finished Lens)

Stock Lens (Finished Lens)

These lenses are pre-manufactured with a certain base curve, prescription and blank size. The lenses are then coated with anti-glare, anti-scratch or both(As per customer requirement), and packaged for retail.

RX Lens (Semi Finished Lens)

Semi-finished lens is the raw blank lens used to produce the most individualized RX lens according to the customer’s requirement. Different prescription powers request for different semi-finished lens types or base curves, it goes through multiple processes (Surfacing, Coating, Tinting etc) Depending on requirement of particular order.

    Lens Production

    Lens production involves a series of steps that transform raw materials into a finished lens product. after receiving the blank lenses from the inventory, the next step is Surfacing.

    Surfacing

    Surfacing is the process by which prescription is placed onto a lens and preparing its surface prior to being cut for the frame.

    There are two types of techniques used for surfacing

    1. Traditional Surfacing
    2. Digital Surfacing

    Traditional surfacing techniques involve the process of grinding and polishing the lenses to their desired prescription and finish. The process involves shaping the lens using specialized grinding machines and polishing it to remove any roughness or imperfections on the surface.

    Digital Surfacing is a lens making process that removes the hard tooling limitations of traditional surfacing. with digital surfacing, labs can produce unlimited shapes and exceptional accuracy on one or two surfaces of the lens to achieve the intended design.

    Surfacing is done in following steps:

    1. Calculation
    2. Unpacking and Blank checking
    3. Taping
    4. Blocking
    5. Generation
    6. Engraving (In Digital Surfacing)
    7. Smoothing (In Traditional Surfacing)
    8. Polishing
    9. D-Blocking

    Lens Calculation system

    Calculating the Precal and Non Precal order, Precal is a lens calculation process which predetermines the thickness of a glazed lens, based on frame shape, prescription and centration. Precal calculates the minimum centre thickness to which a lens can be surfaced to provide the thickness appropriate to the lens/ mount combination. The Precal calculation will only be applied when specified by the customer or when stock lenses cannot be used due to the frame type e.g. semi-rimless (supra) or rimless mounts.

    Unpacking and Blank Checking

    • Check Jobslip description matches lens box label
    • Check TYPE OF LENS, BASE CURVE and ADD POWER
    • Take lenses out of boxes and put lens blanks in the job tray
    • Check for any DEFECT in the blank, If defect found return the lens to stores (check transition lens Colour before taping)
    • Priority for Urgent and Rework orders

    Taping

    • A special surface saver tape is applied on to the front surface of the lens to prevent it from any damage
    • Ensure tape is wrinkle free and firmly in contact with lens at the edge

    Blocking

    • After Taping the semi finished lens is then blocked on CNC blockers using low melting alloys
    • Blocking required for holding the semi finished blank with the chuck holder for further process

    Generation

    • Here the semi finished lens is cut to its final curve and thickness
    • This operation is done on a machine called generator.
    • This is a versatile machine, which can cut the desired curve on the semi finished lens surface, fast and accurate.
    • Generation is a critical step in the production of lenses, as it establishes the initial curvature and prescription of the lens and sets the foundation for the remaining steps in the manufacturing process.

    Engraving (In Digital Surfacing)

    • It is a versatile and precise technique that is used to add a variety of visual and functional elements to lenses during the manufacturing process.
    • CO2 Laser engraving machine providing the customize engraving and logo
    • CO2 laser engraving is used in lens production for a variety of purposes, including adding visual designs and functional markings to the lens surface.

    Smoothing (In Traditional Surfacing)

    • Smoothing is an important step in traditional surfacing in lens production that involves refining the surface of the lens to achieve the desired shape and smoothness. This step is typically performed after the lens has been ground to the correct curvature and prescription.
    • Smoothing is removing the grinding marks from the surface of the semi finished lens without changing its curvature. Here the lens is brought to the exact curve required and surface is smoothened not to have any mark or scratches.
    • The goal of the smoothing process is to achieve a smooth and uniform surface on the lens, with no visible scratches, pits, or other imperfections that could affect the visual quality of the final product. This step is critical to achieving a high-quality lens that meets the required specifications for the prescription and curvature.

    Polishing

    • After the lens is generated and fine ground, it undergoes polishing. Depending on how precise the generation is, the lens go through several stages of polishing
    • 6 Axis Polishing machine is used for final surface polishing
    • Once the polishing process is complete, the lens surface is thoroughly cleaned and inspected to ensure that it meets the required specifications for the prescription and curvature. Any remaining defects or imperfections may be addressed through additional polishing or other corrective measures.

    D-Blocking and Lens Cleaning

    • Remove the finished lens with chuck holder
    • After D-Blocking the final Cleaning and checking is done
    • The final cleaning step is critical to ensure that the lenses are free of any contaminants. By carefully following the proper cleaning procedures, we can ensure that the lenses meet the required specifications and provide optimal visual quality for the end user.

    Quality Control

    In lens production, there are typically two main stages of quality control (QC): surfacing QC and final QC.

    Pre QC/Surfacing QC

    Surfacing QC involves inspecting the lens after it has been ground and polished to its final shape.

    During this process, the lens undergoes three steps:

    • Lens Checking
    • Cosmetics & Surface check
    • Segregation

      Each lenses undergoes pre quality checks after surfacing to ensure all quality parameters as per ISO 14889 & 8980 STDs. In Quality VRX uses high precession & reliable instruments like Focovision & Automapper from Automation & Robotics and LM600 from Nidek.

The inbuilt Dual Mapping is important for inspection of whole surface and meaning full process control. It works by Reflection i.e. Lens Positioning & Tilt & by Transmission for Optical mapping for accurate inspection of Single Vision & Progressive lenses. This ensure utmost performance of lens designs.

    • Cosmetics & Surface check

      After Lens checking, lenses goes for Cosmetics & Surface checks to confirm each lenses qualifies as per Essilor's cosmetic standards.

    • Segregation:

      Finally lenses are segregated based on Value addition, Coating & service wise to transfer to next process.

    Tint

    Tinting is the process of adding a color or shading to the lens material to achieve specific optical properties or cosmetic effects. Tinting is done on lenses for UV protection and to protect eyes from glare of sun, tinting can also be used for fashion purposes or to help individuals with certain eye conditions, such as light sensitivity.

    In the tinting process chemical (Dye) are prepared and the process takes place in hot bath of dyes along with lenses to obtain the color as required. Lenses undergo dyeing in dark colors and the colored lenses protect the glare of sun from reaching to the eyes.

    Types of Tint

    Now a day’s lots of various colors and patterns are being required by customers often for aesthetic requirement.

    • Solid:- Single color in full lens
    • Gradient :- Partial Color
    • Bi-Color:- Two colors in one lens
    • Special Tint: - Lenses made as per customer requirement for color.
    • Tri-Color: - Lens have band of color at top & bottom, the middle part of the lens is kept clear.
    • UV Process Tinting

    Material on which tinting is done-Index

    • 1.5
    • High Index (1.6 & 1.67)
    • Poly
    • Trivex
    • 1.74

    Procedure

    The Tinting of lenses is a wet & hot process in which the lenses are immersed in hot liquid dyes, the heat opens the small pores of the lenses and colored dyed particles impregnate the surface of the lenses, making the lenses to achieve the color of the dyes.

    After tinting of lenses, colored lenses are compared with a pre- approved sample to ensure it has been made as per requirement.

    Procedure for Rx/ Stock Tinting

    Rx/ Stock tinting refers to the process of adding color to a lens to change its appearance or to enhance its performance. The procedure for Rx/ Stock tinting generally involves the following steps:

    • Preparation of Machine

      Pre- Heat clean machine tanks filled with water for 30 mins → Tank temperature should reach 80°c, for adding the color and take care to stir the water to mix the dyes for homogeneity of color & machine temperature should reach at 93°c to start the production (checked with digital thermometer) → Detergent tank is cleaned in every shift.

    • Lens Preparation

      Cosmetic Check of all incoming lens is done in Tint before production starts → Color wise segregation of material → Color wise batch preparation as per basket size/catcher → all lenses are cleaned in detergent bath for 10 Seconds (Commercial detergent- Fena Brand) → all lenses are cleaned in DI water for around 5 Sec→ processed for doing tinting

    • Solid color For Coated Orders

      Lens are dipped for 15-20 mins in the hot bath for the respective color (Temp- 93 °c & above) → they are checked for shade %, if within tolerance then that go for pair matching of individual order in BPI machine manually. Coated orders should have additional tinting around 4%-5%, as leaching of tint color take place in hard coat

      If shade is not achieved

      Lenses dipped again in hot bath till it achieves the nearest possible shade.

    • Solid color for un-coated Progressive Orders

      Lens are dipped for 15-20 mins in the hot bath for the respective color (Temp- 93 °c & above)

    • Gradient color for Coated Lenses (H&K Machine)

      Chosen the appropriate software controlled movement of lens in the H & K machine → Lenses are dipped in hot bath by the mechanical arm of the machine in controlled way so that lens is dipped in the bath as per the desired gradient shade (gradient %) for 25-30 mins → all lenses will go for shade matching at manual BPI machine.

    • Gradient color for Un-Coated Progressive Lenses (H&K Machine)

      Chosen the appropriate software controlled movement of lens in the H & K machine → Lenses are dipped in hot bath by the mechanical arm of the machine in controlled way so that lens is dipped in the bath as per the desired gradient shade (gradient %) for 25-30 mins → all lenses will go for shade matching at manual BPI machine → Send lenses for QC check for shade matching → After tint QC, Lenses will come back to tint production & clean all lenses with IPA → Put all uncoated progressive lenses in thermal oven for curing at 100°c for 1 hour → Pull out the lenses from oven & keep them at room temperature for 1 hour for ambient cooling of lenses → All lenses are sent for uncoated packing.

    Coating

    In the lens manufacturing process, coating refers to the application of thin, transparent layers of material onto the surface of the lens. Coatings are used in lens manufacturing for a variety of reasons, including to enhance the performance and durability of the lens.

    Some common types of lens coatings include Hard (scratch-resistant) coatings, which make the lens more durable and resistant to damage, and Anti-Reflective coatings, which reduce glare and improve visual clarity.

    Hard (Scratch-resistant) Coating

    Scratch resistant coating or hard coating provide lenses with durability and increased longevity. A high quality thermally cured hardcoat provides the best scratch resistance and provides for the most durable ingredient for an AR coating application. Plastic lens materials scratch easily than glass materials, and therefore benefit greatly with application of a durable hardcoat.

    Procedure

    • Lens received from Surface QC/Tint/Finish Store

      As per the requirement lenses comes from different departments for hard coating process.

      Some stocks lenses comes directly from Finish store, some lenses comes from Surface QC or from Tint department

    • Index/Coating wise segregation

      • Lenses are segregated according to the index of the lens or the type of coating to be done on them. Lens index is a number that describes how thick or thin your lenses are. The higher a lens' index is, the thinner it becomes. Higher prescriptions require higher index lenses, while lower prescriptions require lower index lenses.

    • Lens cleaning and Lens loading in lensholder

      The surface of the lens is cleaned and prepared to remove any dirt, oil, or other contaminants that may interfere with the hard coating process. This step is important to ensure that the coating adheres properly to the lens surface. then the lens is loaded into lensholader

    • Lens loading in machine

      The lens is then loaded into a specialized vacuum chamber that is designed for hard coating. The chamber is sealed and the air is evacuated to create a vacuum.

    • Deposition

      The hard coating material is then vaporized inside the vacuum chamber using an electric charge or a heat source. The vaporized material condenses onto the surface of the lens to form a thin, uniform layer.

    • Lens unloading

      Lens unloading typically involves removing the coated lens from the vacuum chamber after the coating has been applied.

    • Lens curing in oven

      The coated lens is then heated to a specific temperature for a specific time period to allow the coating to settle and cure properly. Lens curing process helps to ensure that the hard coating adheres properly to the surface of the lens and achieves the desired level of hardness.

    • Inspection

      Once the hard coating process is complete, the coated lens is inspected for any defects, such as bubbles or unevenness in the coating. The coating thickness is also checked to ensure that it meets the desired specifications.

    • Transfer as per coating

      If Hard Multi-Coating is required, the lens is sent to a multi coating lab.

      If Hard Multi-Coating is not required, the lens is sent directly to the final QC.

    Hard Multi Coating

    In lens production, HMC (Hard Multi-Coat) is a type of coating that is commonly applied to the surface of lenses. HMC coatings can improve the visual performance of eyeglasses by reducing unwanted reflections, and improving contrast.

    Anti-Reflection Coating

    • An Anti-reflection coating is a type of optical coating applied to the surface of lenses to reduce reflection. The primary benefit is the elimination of the reflection itself which makes the eyes of the wearer more visible to others.

    • Another benefit of AR coating is to reduce glare of light, which is particularly noticeable when driving at night or working in front of a computer monitor. The decreased glare means that wearers often find their eyes are less tired, particularly at the end of the day.

    • Allowing more light to pass through the lens also increases contrast and therefore increases visual clarity.

    Additional Benefits

    • Anti-reflection lenses include an additional coating that repels water and oil, making them easier to keep clean.

    • Present Anti Reflection coatings are also having an Anti-Static property which repels dusts and in turn generates less scratch while cleaning.

    Procedure

    • To apply the AR Coating, lenses must be with Hard Coating (Anti Scratch coating) which acts as a buffer cushioning layer.

    • This coating is getting applied on lenses inside a high vacuum chamber. An Ion Gun is used to make the lenses surface rough. This roughening is needed for good adhesion of AR coating.

    • Then some coating materials (Silicon Oxide, Zirconium Oxide, Indium Tin Oxide etc.) gets deposited in alternate layers to achieve the required properties. At last the hydrophobic material gets deposited.

    Step by Step Procedure

    1. Recieve Lenses from Hard Coating Lab
    2. Keep in Thermal Oven at 40 Degree Celsius temperature

    3. Lenses loaded (convex side down) in different sectors with rings

    4. Putting these sectors inside the AR machine
    5. Filling of required materials as per type of AR process ( Convex side )
    6. Start the coating process
    7. After completion of CX side take out sectors from AR Machine
    8. Flipping: The lens is typically held in a specialized fixture or tool that allows it to be flipped over without touching the surface of the lens. The coating is then applied to the other side of the lens.
    9. Putting sectors inside machine
    10. Filling consumable and start respective concave coating process
    11. After completion of concave coating take out lenses from machine chamber
    12. Unloading as per standard practice
    13. Pass lenses to Coating QC
